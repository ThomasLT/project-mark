Safe loading and unloading

Loading and unloading vehicles can be dangerous with a whole host of potential hazards such as people or loads falling from vehicles, people being struck by a load during mechanical handling or people being struck by vehicles. Employers have a legal duty to ensure that the health and safety of their employees, contractors and members of the public are not put at risk as a result of the work they do.

To help Minimise these risks, employers can follow a simple four-step best practice process as a guide to successfully managing safety when working at height:

  1. Plan to avoid work at height where possible
  2. If this is not possible, use work equipment to prevent falls, with the first choice being vehicle-based systems and second choice on-site systems

  4. Always consider measures that protect everyone at risk (e.g. platforms and guardrails) before measures that only protect the individual (e.g. safety harness)
